2.	Room setup and resources 
a.	Room layout: Clusters/stations per group around the room perimeter to allow circulation. 
b.	Power and connectivity: 
i.	Request power strips and extension cords as needed (provide counts in advance). 
ii.	Verify Wi-Fi access requirements; request temporary access points or hotspot allowances if needed. Have an offline fallback. 
c.	Noise/RF considerations: 
i.	If multiple groups use the same band (e.g., BLE/Wi-Fi/802.15.4), plan channel assignments to minimize interference. Have a backup channel plan. 
d.	Check-in and prep: 
i.	Arrive by 5:45 pm for setup and quick tech checks (power, firmware, network). 
ii.	A 10–15 minute instructor walk-through at 6:00 pm to verify readiness. e. Rotation plan: 
i.	Members from every group should rotate to visit other groups. Ensure everyone can independently run and explain the demo. 
ii.	Suggested rotation: around 5 minutes per demo (we have a total of 13 groups, try to go through each one); swap roles so that at least one member present at the station at all times while others visit. 
f. Contingency: 
i.	Posters and demo videos are helpful but not required. Bring a short offline video (1–2 minutes) as a fallback if wireless conditions fail. 
ii.	Prepare pre-recorded data traces and screenshots to demonstrate functionality if live sensing/links degrade. 
3.	Content expectations for live demo 
a.	Explain: 
i.	The idea/problem/use-case and why it matters. 
ii.	System architecture: hardware, firmware, wireless link, and any cloud/backend components. 
iii.	Key design choices (protocols, MCU, sensor selection, power budget). 
iv.	Performance: latency, throughput, range, energy, reliability; measurement methodology. 
v.	Limitations and future work. 
b.	Show: 
i.	Real-time operation under representative conditions. 
ii.	A brief robustness test: how it behaves with interference, movement, or power cycling. iii. A brief power/energy demo or estimate (e.g., current draw at idle/active), if applicable.

b.	Cables, chargers, power strips, batteries, and a safe battery case. 
c.	Laptops with toolchains, terminal/flash tools, and necessary drivers pre-installed. 
d.	Adapters/dongles, debug tools. 
e.	Labels and quick reference cards describing your station (project title, team, wireless tech, key metrics). 
5.	Communication to instructor (needs/requests) 
a.	By May 2, 5:00 pm, email: 
i.	Power strip count and outlet needs. 
ii.	Poster stand. iii. RF/environment needs (quiet RF corner, ethernet drop). 
iv. Accessibility or special scheduling requests.
